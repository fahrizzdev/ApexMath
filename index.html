If f(x) = 3x² − 2x + 1, what is f'(x)?
A6x − 2 ✓
B3x − 2
C6x²
D6x + 1
✓ Correct — Here's why
Power rule: d/dx[3x²] = 6x, d/dx[−2x] = −2, d/dx[1] = 0. So f'(x) = 6x − 2.
